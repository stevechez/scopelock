## Introduction

Effectively managing change orders is crucial for protecting your work and ensuring you get paid for any additional services. BuildRail's **ScopeLock** feature provides pre-vetted legal language within its change order templates to protect your interests. This article outlines the key components of this language and how to leverage it effectively.

## Key Components of Change Order Legal Language

### 1. **Scope of Work**

The language clearly defines any additional work beyond the original contract. This ensures that both parties have a mutual understanding of what is included in the change order.

- **Detailed Descriptions**: Each item should be described in detail to avoid ambiguity.
- **Inclusions/Exclusions**: Clearly list what is and isn't included.

### 2. **Payment Terms**

Outlines how and when payments for additional work will be made, minimizing potential disputes.

- **Payment Schedule**: Specify when payments are due (e.g., upon completion, milestone-based).
- **Late Payment Penalties**: Include language that specifies penalties for late payments.

### 3. **Time Extensions**

Addresses any extensions to the project timeline required by the change order.

- **Revised Timeline**: Clearly state any new deadlines.
- **Impact on Overall Project**: Explain how these changes affect the overall project schedule.

### 4. **Authorization and Signatures**

Ensures that all changes are agreed upon and authorized by both parties.

- **Signatures Required**: Confirm the necessity of signatures from both parties.
- **Date of Authorization**: Include a field for the date to validate the timing of the agreement.

## How to Use the Pre-Vetted Legal Language

### Step-by-Step Instructions

1. **Access the Template**:
   - Navigate to the **ScopeLock** section within your BuildRail dashboard.
   - Select the option to create a new change order.

2. **Customize the Template**:
   - Fill in the necessary details specific to your project. Ensure all fields are completed accurately.
   - Review the pre-vetted legal language sections to ensure they fit your particular situation.

3. **Review and Edit**:
   - Double-check all entries for accuracy and completeness.
   - Utilize the pre-vetted language as a base. Make adjustments where necessary, but be cautious not to remove critical legal protections.

4. **Obtain Signatures**:
   - Ensure both parties review and sign the change order.
   - Use digital signatures through BuildRail for efficiency and to maintain records.

5. **Archive the Change Order**:
   - Once signed, archive the document within BuildRail for easy future reference.

> **Pro Tip**: Always communicate with clients about any additional work and ensure they understand the scope and financial implications before proceeding with changes.
