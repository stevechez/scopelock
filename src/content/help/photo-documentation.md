## Introduction
Uploading site condition photos directly into a ScopeLock agreement is essential for justifying necessary change orders. This feature allows you to provide visual evidence that supports the need for adjustments in the project scope, ensuring transparency and clarity with your clients.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Access Your ScopeLock Agreement

1. **Log into BuildRail**: Use your credentials to access your BuildRail account.
2. **Navigate to ScopeLock**: From the dashboard, click on the **ScopeLock** module to access your existing agreements.
3. **Select an Agreement**: Choose the specific agreement you want to update from the list of active agreements.

### Step 2: Prepare to Upload Photos

1. **Open the Agreement**: Click on the agreement to open its detailed view.
2. **Locate the Photos Section**: Scroll to the section labeled **"Site Condition Photos"** or similar.

### Step 3: Upload Photos

1. **Click on 'Upload Photos'**: This button is typically found within the photos section of the agreement.
2. **Select Photos**: In the dialog box, click **'Choose Files'** and select the photos from your device that you wish to upload. You can upload multiple files simultaneously.
3. **Add Descriptions**: For each photo, provide a brief description to explain the site condition and its impact on the project scope.

### Step 4: Review and Confirm

1. **Review Uploaded Photos**: Ensure that all photos are correctly uploaded and the descriptions accurately describe the site conditions.
2. **Save Changes**: Click **'Save'** or **'Confirm'** to ensure the photos are attached to the agreement.

## Finalizing the Change Order

- **Create a Change Order**: If not already created, initiate a change order that includes the uploaded photos as part of the documentation.
- **Notify Stakeholders**: Use the **Comm Vault** to inform all relevant parties about the change order and direct them to review the photos in the agreement.

> **Pro Tip:** Always capture high-resolution images and provide clear, concise descriptions. This not only justifies the change order but also enhances trust and communication with your client.

## Conclusion
By following these steps, you can effectively upload site condition photos into a ScopeLock agreement, ensuring that your change orders are well-documented and justified. This process is crucial for maintaining project integrity and fostering transparent communication with clients.
