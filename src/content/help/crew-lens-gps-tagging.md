## Introduction

As a contractor, ensuring that job site photos are taken at the correct location is crucial for maintaining project integrity and accountability. SmCrewLens, a feature of BuildRail, provides metadata that can help you verify the authenticity of photos. This guide will walk you through the process of verifying job site photos using SmCrewLens metadata.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Accessing SmCrewLens Metadata

1. **Log into BuildRail:** Start by logging into your BuildRail account.
2. **Navigate to Site Engine:** From the dashboard, go to the Site Engine section where your project details are listed.
3. **Select the Project:** Choose the specific project for which you want to verify the job site photos.

### Step 2: Viewing the Photos

1. **Open the Photo Gallery:** In your selected project, locate the photo gallery where all site images are stored.
2. **Select a Photo:** Click on the photo you wish to verify to open it in full view.

### Step 3: Access Metadata Details

1. **View Metadata:** With the photo open, look for the ‘Metadata’ or ‘Details’ option—usually located near the photo or in a sidebar.
2. **Check Location Data:** Once the metadata is displayed, check the GPS coordinates and address information.

### Step 4: Verify the Address

1. **Compare with Project Address:** Cross-reference the GPS coordinates and address from the metadata with the address listed in your project details.
2. **Use Mapping Tools:** If necessary, input the GPS coordinates into a mapping tool (e.g., Google Maps) to visually confirm the location matches the project address.

> **Pro Tip:** Always ensure your team's devices have location services enabled to capture accurate metadata.

### Step 5: Document Your Findings

1. **Record Verification:** Document your findings in the project notes for accountability. Note any discrepancies or confirmations.
2. **Communicate with Team:** If any discrepancies are found, communicate immediately with your team to resolve potential issues.

## Conclusion

Using SmCrewLens metadata to verify job site photos is an effective way to maintain transparency and accuracy in your projects. By following these steps, you can ensure that your documentation is reliable and that all photos are taken at the correct locations. This not only helps in project management but also boosts client trust and satisfaction.

Remember, consistent verification of metadata can save you time and mitigate disputes in the long run.
