## Introduction

To protect your business and ensure clear communication with clients, it's essential to have a system in place for authorizing out-of-scope work. BuildRail's **ScopeLock** feature allows you to require mandatory digital signatures from clients before starting any additional work. This article provides a straightforward guide to implementing this process.

## Why Require Digital Signatures?

Requiring digital signatures for out-of-scope work ensures:

- **Clear Communication**: Clients understand and agree to changes.
- **Legal Protection**: Signed agreements provide a legal record.
- **Prevent Free Work**: Ensures all work is compensated.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Access ScopeLock

1. **Log in to BuildRail** using your contractor credentials.
2. Navigate to the **ScopeLock** section from the dashboard.

### Step 2: Configure Change Order Settings

1. **Click on "Settings"** in the ScopeLock dashboard.
2. Enable the option for **Mandatory Digital Signatures** for all change orders.

### Step 3: Create a Change Order

1. Go to the **Projects** tab and select the project requiring a change order.
2. Click on **"Create Change Order"**.
3. Enter the details of the out-of-scope work:
   - **Description** of the work.
   - **Cost** estimate.
   - **Expected Completion Date**.

### Step 4: Send for Client Approval

1. Once the change order is created, click on **"Send for Approval"**.
2. The client will receive an email with the details of the change order.
3. Instruct the client to review and digitally sign the document.

### Step 5: Monitor Approval Status

1. Return to the ScopeLock dashboard.
2. Check the status of the change order under **Pending Approvals**.
3. Follow up with the client if necessary to expedite the process.

### Step 6: Proceed with Work

- Once the digital signature is received, you can confidently proceed with the out-of-scope work.

> **Pro Tip**: Always keep a record of signed change orders. They not only provide clarity but also serve as a valuable reference for future projects.

## Conclusion

Implementing mandatory digital signatures for out-of-scope work is a critical step in protecting your business interests and maintaining clear communication with clients. By following the steps outlined above, you can ensure all additional work is authorized and accounted for.
