## Introduction

Change orders are inevitable in construction projects. Properly managing them is crucial to prevent scope creep, which can lead to unpaid work and disrupted timelines. BuildRail's **ScopeLock** feature allows you to approve change orders efficiently and utilize digital signatures to keep your projects on track.

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Accessing Change Orders

1. **Log in to BuildRail.**
2. Navigate to **ScopeLock** from your dashboard.
3. Select the project requiring a change order review.

### Step 2: Reviewing Change Orders

1. Within the project, find the **Change Orders** tab.
2. Review the details of the submitted change order, including:
   - **Description** of the change
   - **Cost Implications**
   - **Timeline Adjustments**

### Step 3: Approving Change Orders

1. If the change order details are satisfactory, click **Approve**.
2. **Add Comments** if necessary, for the contractor or client.

> **Pro Tip:** Always ensure all financial and timeline impacts are clear before approval to avoid disputes later.

### Step 4: Using Digital Signatures

1. After approval, proceed to the **Signatures** section within the change order.
2. Click **Request Signature** to notify relevant parties (e.g., clients, subcontractors).
3. Once all parties have signed, the change order status will update to **Approved and Signed**.

### Step 5: Finalizing the Change Order

1. Confirm the change order is now part of the project's official scope.
2. Communicate any changes in tasks or timelines to your team to ensure seamless execution.

## Conclusion

By using BuildRail's ScopeLock and digital signatures, you effectively manage change orders, minimizing scope creep and protecting your profits. Always verify the details of each change order and maintain clear communication with all stakeholders.

> **Pro Tip:** Regularly review your project's change orders to anticipate potential impacts on your timeline and budget.

By following these steps, you can ensure that all changes are accurately documented and agreed upon, keeping your projects running smoothly and profitably.
